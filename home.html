Introduction

  • Title 10 of the United States Code outlines the role of the U.S. armed forces in the United States Code. The current title 10 is a combination of the former title 10 and title 32 into one title by an act of Congress on August 10, 1956. As stated “It provides the legal basis for the roles, missions and organization of each of the services as well as the United States Department of Defense”(Wiki). Title 10 of the United States Code is separated into 5 components. These components are general military law, army, navy and marine corps, air force and space corps, and the reserve components.

General Military Law

  • The General Military Law section of Title 10 is about the general law throughout the whole entire military and has 389 chapters. Originally created on June 30, 1775 and was later ratified in 1788. This is broken up into 4 sections. These are general military powers, personnel, training and education, and service supply and property. General military powers has 24 chapters and is everything from general powers and functions of the military to budgeting and defense intelligence. Personnel consists of 57 chapters and is the standard for all military personnel. This includes active duty to reserve and general service requirements and retirement/term contract. Training and education has 14 chapters and is about military personnel getting on site training in order for them to do their military job as well as college options for them to succeed beyond their military service. Service, supply, and property is divided into 2 sections. The first contains 23 chapters and the second one contains 188 chapters. Service, supply, and property is all about costs of manufacturing weapons and housing for the military.

Army

  • The Army section of Title 10 is about the standards of the U.S. Army and has 82 chapters. The Department of War was originally formed in 1789 as an Executive Department of the United States and was split into the Department of the Army on September 18, 1947. This is broken up into 4 sections. These are organization, personnel, training, and service supply and property. Organization has 4 chapters and contains information about the department of the army, the army staff, and the general army. Personnel is about strength, enlistment, rank and command, and the overall strength and size of the army. Training contains training generally, United States military academy, and the schools and camps. Lastly, the service supply and property is all about costs of manufacturing weapons and housing for the military.
