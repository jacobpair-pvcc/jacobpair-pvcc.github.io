Navy and Marine Corps

  • The Navy and Marine Corps section of Title 10 is about the standards of the U.S. Navy and Marine Corps and has 96 chapters. It was established by an Act of Congress on 30 April 1798, at the urging of Secretary of War James McHenry, to provide a government organizational structure to the United States Navy. This is broken up into 4 sections. These are organization, personnel, education and training, and general administration. Organization has 6 chapters and contains information about the department of the navy, the department of the marine corps, and the Office of the Chief of Naval Operations. Personnel has 36 chapters and is about strength, enlistment, rank and command, and the overall strength and size of the Navy and Marine Corps together. Education and training has 5 chapters and is about military personnel getting on site training in order for them to do their military job as well as college options for them to succeed beyond their military service. General administration is about quarters, utilities, and services as well as vessels and facilities.

Air Force and Space Corps

  • The Air Force and Space Corps section of Title 10 is about the standards of the U.S. Air Force and Space Corps and has 82 chapters. It was formed on September 18, 1947. This is broken up into 4 sections. These are organization, personnel, training, and service supply and procurement. Organization has 5 chapters that contain the Department of the Air Force, The Air Staff, The general Air Force, and Space Force. Personnel has 17 chapters and is about strength, enlistment, rank and command, and the overall strength and size of the Air Force and Space Corps together. Training has 14 chapters and is about military personnel getting on site training in order for them to do their military job. Service supply and procurement is about fleet size, transportation, and utilities and services.

Reserve Components

  • The Reserve Components section of Title 10 is about the standards of the U.S. Reserve and has 804 chapters. This is broken up into 5 sections. These are organization, personnel generally, promotion and retention of officers on the reserve active-status list, training for reserve components and educational assistance programs, and service supply and property. Organization has 8 chapters and is all about reserve components generally, elements of reserve components, reserve component commands, administration of reserve components. Personnel generally has 16 chapters and is all about strength, enlistment, rank and command, and the overall strength and size of the reserves. Promotion and retention of officers on the reserve active-status list has 6 chapters and is all about applicability and reserve active-status lists, selection boards, and promotion qualifications. Training for reserve components and educational assistance programs has 6 chapters and is about Educational Assistance for Reserve Component Members Supporting Contingency Operations and Certain Other Operations. Service supply and property has 3 chapters and is all about transportation, and utilities and services.
